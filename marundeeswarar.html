HISTORY

Marundeeswarar Temple is a temple dedicated to Hindu deity Shiva, located in Thiruvanmiyur, Chennai adjacent to the beach of Bay of Bengal. It is one of the 275 Paadal Petra Sthalams where two of the most revered Nayanars (Saivite Saints), Appar and Tirugnana Sambandar, have glorified the temple with their verses during the 7th century CE. The temple has been widely expanded by Chola kings during the 11th century CE.[2] The temple has two seven-tiered gateway towers, a huge tank, with the overall temple area covering 1 acre. The Marundeeswarar temple has been a place of curative worship for people with diseases. The temple has six daily rituals at various times from 5:30 a.m. to 10 p.m., and twelve yearly festivals on its calendar. The present masonry structure was built during the Chola dynasty in the 9th century, while later expansions are attributed to Vijayanagar rulers.

WAYS TO REACH

By Car/Taxi: If you have your own vehicle or prefer a taxi, you can reach the temple by road. Use GPS or navigation apps to find the best route. The temple has parking facilities available nearby.

By Bus: Chennai has an extensive public bus network operated by the Metropolitan Transport Corporation (MTC). You can check the MTC website or inquire at the nearest bus station for the routes that pass through Thiruvanmiyur and alight at Marundeeswarar Temple bus stop.

By Metro: Chennai Metro Rail provides a convenient option to reach Marundeeswarar Temple. You can take the metro and get down at the "Thiruvanmiyur" metro station, which is located near the temple. From there, you can either walk or take a short auto-rickshaw ride to reach the temple.

By Train: The nearest railway station to Marundeeswarar Temple is the Thiruvanmiyur Railway Station. From there, you can hire a taxi or take an auto-rickshaw to reach the temple, which is approximately 1 kilometer away.

By Auto-Rickshaw: Auto-rickshaws are readily available in Chennai. You can hire an auto-rickshaw from any part of the city and ask the driver to take you to Marundeeswarar Temple in Thiruvanmiyur.
