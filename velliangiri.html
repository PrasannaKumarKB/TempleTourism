Velliangiri Temple

About Velliangiri Temple

Spiritual Abode of Lord Shiva

Velliangiri Temple, located at the foothills of the Velliangiri Mountains near Coimbatore, is a revered shrine dedicated to Lord Shiva. Known as the "Kailash of the South," this temple is renowned for its spiritual significance and the pilgrimage trek that thousands of devotees undertake to the summit of the Velliangiri Hills. With its deep spiritual roots and breathtaking natural surroundings, the temple has been a major destination for Shiva devotees seeking divine blessings and inner peace.

History

The Velliangiri Temple, located at the foothills of the Velliangiri Mountains near Coimbatore, holds immense spiritual significance. It is considered the "Kailash of the South" and is dedicated to Lord Shiva. The origins of the temple are ancient, with legends stating that Lord Shiva himself meditated on these sacred hills.

The temple attracts thousands of devotees, especially during the Tamil month of Chithirai (April-May), when pilgrims undertake a challenging trek to the summit of the Velliangiri Hills to offer prayers. This trek is believed to cleanse one's sins, and the temple continues to be an important pilgrimage destination for Lord Shiva's devotees.

Ways to Reach

Velliangiri Temple, located near Coimbatore, is accessible by several modes of transportation, making it convenient for both local and international visitors:

  • By Air: Coimbatore International Airport is the nearest airport, situated approximately 35 kilometers from the temple base. From the airport, you can hire a taxi or use public transport to reach the temple.
  • By Train: Coimbatore Junction is the closest major railway station, located about 40 kilometers away. From the station, taxis, buses, and auto-rickshaws are available to reach the temple base.
  • By Bus: Regular bus services operate from Coimbatore city to Poondi, the base of Velliangiri Hills, which is around 35 kilometers from Coimbatore.
  • By Road: The temple is accessible via road from Coimbatore, with good connectivity through taxis, auto-rickshaws, and private vehicles. The temple base, Poondi, is the starting point for the trek up the Velliangiri Hills.

Festivals

Velliangiri Temple is celebrated for its sacred atmosphere and its festivals, which are observed with immense devotion. Some of the major festivals include:

  • Maha Shivaratri: Celebrated in honor of Lord Shiva, Maha Shivaratri is observed with night-long vigils, special poojas, and rituals at the Velliangiri Temple, drawing thousands of devotees to the sacred hills.
  • Karthigai Deepam: Known as the festival of lights, Karthigai Deepam is celebrated by lighting oil lamps throughout the temple and on the hills, symbolizing the divine light of Lord Shiva.
  • Aadi Perukku: Celebrated during the Tamil month of Aadi (July-August), this festival is dedicated to the River Goddess, with special prayers for prosperity and good fortune.
  • Thai Amavasya: A sacred new moon day in the Tamil month of Thai (January-February), devotees trek up the Velliangiri Hills to offer prayers and seek blessings from Lord Shiva.

Pooja Timings

The daily pooja timings at Velliangiri Temple are as follows:

  • Ko Pooja (First Pooja): 5:30 AM - The first pooja of the day, where offerings and prayers are made to Lord Shiva.
  • Kaala Santhi Pooja: 8:00 AM - Morning pooja with rituals and offerings dedicated to the deity.
  • Uchikala Pooja (Noon Pooja): 12:00 PM - Midday pooja where special offerings are made to Lord Shiva.
  • Sayaraksha Pooja (Evening Pooja): 5:00 PM - Evening pooja with aarti and prayers, marking the end of the day.
  • Ardhajama Pooja (Night Pooja): 9:00 PM - The final pooja of the day before the deity is put to rest.

Special poojas and rituals are performed on auspicious days and during festivals, drawing devotees from far and wide to seek blessings from Lord Shiva.
