Panchamukha Anjaneyar Temple

History

The Panchamuga Anjaneyar Temple in RS Puram, Coimbatore, has a deep historical significance. It was established to honor Lord Hanuman in his Panchamukha (five-faced) form, representing his immense power and divine attributes. The temple was built with the devotion of local communities, and over time, it has become a center of spiritual attraction for devotees in the region.

The temple's five-faced idol is believed to be a symbol of protection, devotion, and strength, making it a key spiritual destination for those seeking Lord Hanuman's blessings and grace.

Ways to Reach

Panchamuga Anjaneyar Temple is conveniently accessible by multiple transportation options, making it easy for visitors from nearby areas and afar to reach:

  • By Air: Coimbatore International Airport is the nearest airport, located about 12 kilometers from the temple. From the airport, you can take a taxi or use public transport to reach the temple.
  • By Train: Coimbatore Junction is the nearest major railway station, situated around 4 kilometers from the temple. You can take a taxi, auto-rickshaw, or local bus to reach the temple from the station.
  • By Bus: RS Puram Bus Stop is the closest bus station, located just a kilometer away. Regular bus services from various parts of the city are available to this area.

Festivals

Panchamuga Anjaneyar Temple celebrates several important festivals that attract a large number of devotees. Some of the major festivals include:

  • Hanuman Jayanti: This festival celebrates the birth of Lord Hanuman with special poojas, religious ceremonies, and chanting of Hanuman Chalisa.
  • Rama Navami: Celebrating the birth of Lord Rama, this festival involves grand rituals and poojas, especially because of Lord Hanuman's devotion to Rama.
  • Deepavali: The festival of lights is marked by special poojas and lighting of lamps around the temple, symbolizing the victory of good over evil.

Pooja Timings

The daily pooja timings at Panchamuga Anjaneyar Temple are as follows:

  • First Pooja: 6:00 AM - The early morning pooja where prayers and offerings are made to Lord Hanuman.
  • Mid-Morning Pooja: 10:00 AM - A special pooja conducted for devotees who visit in the morning hours.
  • Evening Pooja: 6:00 PM - A grand aarti and pooja as the sun sets, invoking the blessings of Lord Hanuman.
  • Night Pooja: 8:00 PM - The final pooja of the day before the deity is put to rest for the night.

On festival days and auspicious occasions, special poojas and rituals are performed to celebrate the glory of Lord Hanuman.
