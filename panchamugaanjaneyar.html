HISTORY

The Panchamukha Anjaneyar Temple in Coimbatore is a divine shrine dedicated to Lord Hanuman. The temple is renowned for its unique feature of Lord Hanuman with five faces (panchamukha). Each face represents a different aspect of Lord Hanuman, symbolizing strength, wisdom, devotion, courage, and righteousness. The temple is adorned with intricate carvings and beautiful sculptures that depict various stories from Hindu mythology. Devotees visit this temple to seek the blessings of Lord Hanuman and experience spiritual solace. The serene atmosphere and the divine presence make it a significant place of worship in Coimbatore.

WAYS TO REACH

By Air: The nearest airport to Coimbatore is Coimbatore International Airport.From the airport, you can hire a taxi or take a bus to reach Panchamukha Anjaneyar Temple, which is approximately 14 kilometers away.

By Train: Coimbatore Junction is the main railway station in Coimbatore.From the railway station, you can hire a taxi, take an auto-rickshaw, or use public transportation to reach Panchamukha Anjaneyar Temple, which is approximately 11 kilometers away.

By Road: Coimbatore is well-connected by road, and you can reach the city by bus or private vehicle.From Coimbatore, you can take the Avinashi Road (NH 47) towards Tiruppur.Panchamukha Anjaneyar Temple is located on Avinashi Road, approximately 11 kilometers from the city center.

By Taxi or Auto-Rickshaw: You can hire a taxi, take an auto-rickshaw, or use public transportation to reach the temple.
