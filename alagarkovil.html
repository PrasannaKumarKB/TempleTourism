HISTORY

Alagar Koyil is a village in Madurai district in the South Indian state of Tamil Nadu. The history and living of the village is centered around Kallalagar Temple. Constructed in the Dravidian style of architecture, the temple is glorified in the Nalayira Divya Prabandham, the early medieval Tamil canon of the Alvar saints from the 6thâ€“9th centuries CE. It is one of the 108 Divya Desams dedicated to Vishnu, who is worshiped as Kallalagar and his consort Lakshmi as Thirumamagal.

WAYS TO REACH

By Air: The nearest airport to Madurai is Madurai International Airport, located about 15 kilometers away from the city center. From the airport, you can hire a taxi or take a pre-paid taxi to reach Kallalazhagar Temple.

By Train: Madurai Junction is the main railway station in Madurai and is well-connected to various cities across the country. From Madurai Junction, you can hire a taxi, auto-rickshaw, or use public transportation to reach Kallalazhagar Temple, which is located approximately 20 kilometers away in Alagar Kovil.

By Road: Madurai has a well-developed road network, and you can reach the city by bus or car. State-run buses and private buses operate regular services to Madurai from nearby cities and towns. From Madurai, you can take a local bus or hire a taxi to reach Kallalazhagar Temple in Alagar Kovil.

Once you reach Alagar Kovil, you can ask for directions to Kallalazhagar Temple from locals or use GPS navigation for guidance. The temple is a popular pilgrimage site, and you should be able to easily find transportation options to reach there.
