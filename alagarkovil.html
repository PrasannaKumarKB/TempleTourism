Alagar Kovil

History

Alagar Temple, located about 21 kilometers from Madurai, is dedicated to Lord Kallazhagar, an incarnation of Lord Vishnu. The temple is renowned for its exquisite architecture and historical significance, believed to date back to the 7th century. It is a prominent pilgrimage site, especially for devotees of Lord Vishnu.

According to legend, Lord Kallazhagar came to Madurai to attend the wedding of Lord Murugan and Valli, but he did not attend due to a misunderstanding. The temple stands as a reminder of this divine event, and the annual Azhagar Kovil festival attracts thousands of devotees who come to witness the grand procession of the deity.

Ways to Reach

Alagar Temple is well-connected and accessible by various means of transport:

  • By Air: The nearest airport is Madurai International Airport, located approximately 25 kilometers from the temple. Taxis and auto-rickshaws are available for transportation.
  • By Train: Madurai Junction is the closest major railway station, about 20 kilometers away. From there, local transportation such as taxis and buses can be used to reach the temple.
  • By Bus: Several local buses operate from Madurai to Alagar Kovil, making it convenient for visitors to reach the temple.

Festivals

Alagar Temple is celebrated for its vibrant festivals, which draw numerous devotees every year. Some of the significant festivals include:

  • Azhagar Kovil Festival: Celebrated in the Tamil month of Chithirai (April-May), this festival marks the procession of Lord Alagar to Madurai for the wedding of Lord Murugan. The temple is beautifully decorated, and various rituals are performed.
  • Vaikunta Ekadasi: This festival is observed in the month of Margazhi (December-January) and includes special poojas and celebrations dedicated to Lord Vishnu.
  • Navaratri: The nine nights of worship are celebrated with special rituals, music, and dance, attracting large crowds of devotees.

Pooja Timings

The daily pooja timings at Alagar Temple are as follows:

  • Morning Pooja: 6:00 AM - The first pooja of the day, where special offerings are made to Lord Kallazhagar.
  • Afternoon Pooja: 12:00 PM - Midday rituals conducted for devotees visiting the temple.
  • Evening Pooja: 6:00 PM - The evening aarti is performed, invoking the blessings of Lord Vishnu.
  • Night Pooja: 8:00 PM - The final pooja of the day, concluding with prayers before the temple closes.

On festival days, additional poojas and rituals are performed, enhancing the spiritual experience for the devotees.
