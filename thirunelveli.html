T H I R U N E L V E L I

Thirunelveli, also known as Tirunelveli, is a district located in the state of Tamil Nadu. The district is renowned for its ancient temples that showcase the rich cultural and religious heritage of the region. Thirunelveli, with its architectural wonders and spiritual significance, attracts visitors from far and wide. As you explore the district, you will be captivated by the timeless beauty and divine atmosphere of its temples.

Nellaiappar Temple

Nellaiappar Temple, also known as Arulmigu Swami Nellaiappar and Kanthimathi Amman Temple, is a prominent Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Shiva and Goddess Parvati. The temple is famous for its stunning architecture and intricate carvings. It is one of the largest temples in Tamil Nadu and attracts a large number of devotees and tourists.

Kazhugumalai Murugan Temple

Kazhugumalai Murugan Temple is a renowned temple dedicated to Lord Murugan. It is situated on a hill and offers a panoramic view of the surrounding landscape. The temple is known for its rock-cut sculptures and is a significant pilgrimage site for devotees of Lord Murugan.

Sankaranainar Kovil

Sankaranainar Kovil is an ancient temple dedicated to Lord Shiva. The temple is known for its architectural beauty and historical significance. It is believed to be more than 1,000 years old and attracts devotees seeking spiritual blessings.

Thirukutralanathar Temple

Thirukutralanathar Temple, also known as Alwarthirunagari Temple, is a famous temple dedicated to Lord Vishnu. The temple is one of the 108 Divya Desams, the holiest abodes of Lord Vishnu. It is associated with the Tamil poet-saint Nammalvar and attracts devotees from all over the country.

Adi Kumbeswarar Temple

Adi Kumbeswarar Temple is a renowned temple dedicated to Lord Shiva. The temple is one of the Pancha Bhoota Stalam temples, representing the element of water. It is known for its grand architecture and is a significant pilgrimage site in the region.

Srivilliputhur Andal Temple

Srivilliputhur Andal Temple is a renowned temple dedicated to Goddess Andal. The temple is one of the 108 Divya Desams and is famous for its annual festival called "Aadi Pooram." The temple's architecture and the devotion to Goddess Andal make it a significant pilgrimage site.

Kailasanathar Temple, Tenkasi

Kailasanathar Temple in Tenkasi is an ancient Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Shiva. The temple's architecture reflects the Pandya and Nayaka styles of architecture. It is known for its beautiful sculptures and serene ambiance.
