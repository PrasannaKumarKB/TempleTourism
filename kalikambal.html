Kalikambal Temple

About Kalikambal Temple

Historical Temple

Kalikambal Temple, located in the heart of Chennai, is a revered shrine dedicated to Goddess Kalikambal, an incarnation of Goddess Parvati. This ancient temple has a rich history and is known for its beautiful architecture, intricate carvings, and vibrant festivals. Believed to have been constructed in the 17th century, the temple features a stunning gopuram (tower) and is famous for its unique rituals and offerings. It attracts numerous devotees seeking blessings and spiritual solace.

History

Kalikambal Temple has a rich history that dates back to the 17th century when it was originally established to honor Goddess Kalikambal, an incarnation of Goddess Parvati. Over the years, the temple has undergone various renovations and expansions, driven by the devotion of its followers and the support of the local community, solidifying its status as a significant pilgrimage site.

The temple's name, 'Kalikambal,' reflects the goddess's fierce and protective nature. It is renowned for its unique rituals and vibrant festivals, drawing thousands of devotees who visit to seek blessings and participate in the celebrations, particularly during the Navaratri festival.

Ways to Reach

Kalikambal Temple is conveniently accessible by various modes of transportation, ensuring a smooth journey for both local and international visitors:

  • By Air: Chennai International Airport is the nearest airport, located about 18 kilometers from the temple. From the airport, you can hire a taxi or use public transport to reach the temple.
  • By Train: Chennai Egmore Railway Station is the major railhead, situated approximately 4 kilometers from the temple. From the station, you can take a taxi, auto-rickshaw, or use local bus services to reach the temple.
  • By Bus: The nearest bus terminus is the Broadway Bus Stand, which is about 1.5 kilometers from the temple. Regular bus services operate from various parts of the city to the temple area.
  • By Metro: The nearest metro station to the temple is the High Court Metro Station, which is approximately 1 kilometer away. You can either walk or take an auto-rickshaw from the metro station to the temple.

Festivals

Kalikambal Temple is celebrated for its vibrant festivals, observed with great enthusiasm and devotion. Some of the major festivals include:

  • Panguni Uthiram: Celebrated in the Tamil month of Panguni (March-April), this festival involves grand processions, special poojas, and various religious rituals dedicated to Goddess Kalikambal.
  • Navaratri: This nine-day festival dedicated to Goddess Durga is celebrated with special poojas, cultural programs, and beautifully decorated temple interiors, culminating in a grand celebration.
  • Karthigai Deepam: Known as the festival of lights, Karthigai Deepam is celebrated by lighting traditional oil lamps around the temple premises, creating a mesmerizing sight.
  • Thai Poosam: Although primarily dedicated to Lord Murugan, this festival is also observed at Kalikambal Temple with colorful processions and elaborate rituals, drawing large crowds of devotees.

Pooja Timings

The daily pooja timings at Kalikambal Temple are as follows:

  • Ko Pooja (First Pooja): 5:30 AM - The first pooja of the day, where offerings and prayers are made to Goddess Kalikambal.
  • Kaala Santhi Pooja: 8:00 AM - Morning pooja with rituals and offerings dedicated to the deity.
  • Uchikala Pooja (Noon Pooja): 12:00 PM - Midday pooja where special offerings are made to Goddess Kalikambal.
  • Sayaraksha Pooja (Evening Pooja): 5:00 PM - Evening pooja with aarti and prayers as the day comes to an end.
  • Ardhajama Pooja (Night Pooja): 9:00 PM - The final pooja of the day before the deity is put to rest.

Special poojas and rituals are performed on auspicious days and during festivals, attracting large numbers of devotees.
