Thiruttani Temple

About Thiruttani Temple

Historical Temple

Thiruttani Temple, located in the serene hills of Thiruttani near Chennai, is a significant temple dedicated to Lord Murugan. This temple is celebrated for its scenic beauty and vibrant religious festivals, which showcase the rich cultural heritage of Tamil Nadu. Believed to be built over centuries, Thiruttani Temple is distinguished by its majestic gopuram (gateway tower) and exquisite carvings, drawing thousands of devotees and tourists every year.

History

The origins of Thiruttani Temple date back to ancient times, making it one of the most revered shrines dedicated to Lord Murugan. Over the centuries, the temple has undergone several restorations and renovations, with contributions from various dynasties and devotees, establishing its prominence as a key pilgrimage destination.

The temple is located on a hill, symbolizing spiritual elevation, and is one of the six sacred abodes (Arupadai Veedu) of Lord Murugan. Thiruttani is known for its major festivals, especially Skanda Sashti, which draws thousands of devotees seeking divine blessings.

Ways to Reach

Thiruttani Temple is conveniently accessible by various modes of transportation, ensuring a smooth journey for both local and international visitors:

  • By Air: The nearest airport is Chennai International Airport, located approximately 100 kilometers from Thiruttani. From the airport, you can hire a taxi or take a bus to reach Thiruttani.
  • By Train: The nearest railway station is Thiruttani Railway Station, which is well-connected to major cities. From the station, you can take an auto-rickshaw or taxi to reach the temple.
  • By Bus: Thiruttani has a bus terminus with regular services from various parts of Tamil Nadu, making it easy to reach the temple area.
  • By Car: Easily reachable by private vehicles, with ample parking facilities available near the temple.

Festivals

Thiruttani Temple is renowned for its vibrant festivals, celebrated with great fervor and devotion. Some of the major festivals include:

  • Panguni Uthiram: Celebrated in the Tamil month of Panguni (March-April), this festival features grand processions and special poojas dedicated to Lord Murugan.
  • Thai Poosam: This important festival for Lord Murugan is marked by colorful processions and elaborate rituals, attracting large crowds of devotees.
  • Karthigai Deepam: Known as the festival of lights, Karthigai Deepam is celebrated by lighting traditional oil lamps around the temple, creating a beautiful ambiance.
  • Vaikasi Visakam: This festival, which falls in the Tamil month of Vaikasi, is celebrated to honor the birth of Lord Murugan with special rituals and festivities.

Pooja Timings

The daily pooja timings at Thiruttani Temple are as follows:

  • Ko Pooja (First Pooja): 5:30 AM - The first pooja of the day, where offerings and prayers are made to Lord Murugan.
  • Kaala Santhi Pooja: 8:00 AM - Morning pooja with rituals and offerings dedicated to the deity.
  • Uchikala Pooja (Noon Pooja): 12:00 PM - Midday pooja where special offerings are made to Lord Murugan.
  • Sayaraksha Pooja (Evening Pooja): 5:00 PM - Evening pooja with aarti and prayers as the day comes to an end.
  • Ardhajama Pooja (Night Pooja): 9:00 PM - The final pooja of the day before the deity is put to rest.

Special poojas and rituals are performed on auspicious days and during festivals, attracting large numbers of devotees.
