Famous Events and Festivals in Tamil Nadu

Pongal Festival

Pongal is one of the most significant festivals in Tamil Nadu, celebrated in mid-January. It is a harvest festival that marks the beginning of the Tamil month of Thai. During Pongal, people cook a special dish called 'Pongal' using freshly harvested rice, jaggery, and other ingredients. The festival involves various rituals, including decorating cattle, offering prayers, and drawing colorful kolam (rangoli) designs.

Madurai Chithirai Festival

This festival, held in Madurai in April/May, reenacts the marriage of Lord Alagar (Vishnu) and Goddess Meenakshi. The highlight is the procession of Lord Alagar on a golden horse to Madurai from his abode in Azhagar Kovil.

Madurai Meenakshi Temple Festival

The Meenakshi Temple in Madurai hosts an annual 12-day festival in April/May to celebrate the divine wedding of Lord Sundareswarar (Shiva) and Goddess Meenakshi. The temple is beautifully decorated, and the event features processions, cultural performances, and a grand chariot procession.

Aadi Perukku

This is a unique festival celebrated in July/August to pay homage to the water resources in Tamil Nadu. People take a dip in rivers and worship the deities to express gratitude for water's life-sustaining qualities.

Karthigai Deepam

This festival, celebrated in November/December, is known as the "Festival of Lights." People light rows of oil lamps and place them in and around their homes to honor Lord Shiva.

Mahamaham Festival

Held once every 12 years in the temple town of Kumbakonam, this event attracts millions of pilgrims who take a dip in the Mahamaham tank to wash away their sins. It is believed that the tank is connected to the holy rivers of India.

Thai Poosam

Thai Poosam is a Tamil Hindu festival celebrated in January/February to honor Lord Murugan. Devotees carry a kavadi (decorated arch) and perform various rituals, including piercing their bodies with small spears or hooks as an act of devotion.

Navaratri

Navaratri is a nine-night festival celebrated in September or October to honor Goddess Durga. People set up golu (display of dolls), perform traditional dances like Dandiya and Garba, and engage in various cultural activities.
