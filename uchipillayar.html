HISTORY

Perched atop a rock, Rockfort Uchi Pillayar Temple is a famous Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Ganesha. To reach the temple, one has to climb a series of steps carved into the rock, which offers breathtaking views of the city. The temple's location on the rock gives it a unique and majestic appearance. Devotees visit the temple to seek blessings and experience spiritual serenity in the peaceful ambiance. Inside the temple, the main deity, Lord Ganesha, is worshipped with great reverence and devotion.

WAYS TO REACH

By Foot: The Uchi Pillayar Temple is located on top of the Rockfort Hill, and you can reach it by climbing the 437 steps carved into the rock. The starting point for the climb is at the base of the hill, near the Thayumanaswamy Temple.

By Cable Car: There is a cable car facility available to reach the Uchi Pillayar Temple for those who prefer not to climb the steps. The cable car operates from the foothill to the top of the hill, providing a convenient and scenic way to reach the temple.

By Auto-rickshaw or Taxi: If you prefer not to climb or take the cable car, you can hire an auto-rickshaw or a taxi to reach the Uchi Pillayar Temple. The vehicles can take you directly to the entrance of the temple. The starting point for the auto-rickshaws or taxis is usually near the Thayumanaswamy Temple or the Rockfort Bus Stand.

By Bus: Trichy has a well-connected bus network, and you can take a local bus to reach the Rockfort Temple. The buses plying on the routes that pass through the Rockfort area will drop you near the temple entrance. The Rockfort Bus Stand is located nearby, making it convenient for bus travelers.
