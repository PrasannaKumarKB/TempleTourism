HISTORY

Vandiyur Mariamman Teppakulam is a temple tank located near to Vandiyur Mariamman Temple and situated at a distance of about 5 km from the Meenakshi Amman Temple. Literally, Teppakulam means temple pond mainly used for devotional festivals.The tank is connected to Vaigai River through an ingenious system of underground Channels. It has total of 12 long stairs (steps) made of granite on all four sides. The temple as well as the stairs was built by the King Thirumalai Nayak. In the centre of the tank there is a Madapam called Maiya Mandapam (Central Mandapam) with Vinayakar temple and garden.

WAYS TO REACH

By Air: The nearest airport to Madurai is Madurai International Airport, located about 15 kilometers away from the city center. From the airport, you can hire a taxi or take a pre-paid taxi to reach the temple.

By Train: Madurai Junction is a major railway station in South India and is well-connected to various cities across the country. You can take a train to Madurai Junction and then hire a taxi, auto-rickshaw, or use public transportation to reach the temple, which is located in the heart of the city.

By Road: Madurai has a well-developed road network, and you can reach the city by bus or car. State-run buses and private buses operate regular services to Madurai from nearby cities and towns. The temple is located in the center of Madurai, so you can easily reach it by local transport or hire a taxi or auto-rickshaw.

Once you reach Madurai city, Meenakshi Amman Temple is situated in the heart of the city near the famous Meenakshi Sundareswarar Temple. The temple complex is easily accessible by foot, and there are parking facilities available nearby if you are traveling by private vehicle.
