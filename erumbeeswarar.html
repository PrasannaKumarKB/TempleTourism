HISTORY

Erumbeeswarar Temple, also known as Erumbeeswarar Kovil, is a revered Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Shiva. Situated in Trichy, the temple has a rich history and is known for its architectural splendor. The temple is believed to have been built during the Chola dynasty and is considered a sacred pilgrimage site for devotees. The intricate carvings and sculptures on the temple walls depict various mythological stories and add to its grandeur. Devotees throng to the Erumbeeswarar Temple to seek blessings and experience its spiritual ambiance.

WAYS TO REACH

By Auto-rickshaw or Taxi: The most convenient way to reach Erumbeeswarar Temple is by hiring an auto-rickshaw or a taxi. The temple is located in Thiruverumbur, which is approximately 8 kilometers from Trichy. You can find auto-rickshaws and taxis easily in Trichy, and they can take you directly to the temple entrance.

By Bus: Trichy has a well-connected bus network, and you can take a local bus to reach Erumbeeswarar Temple. Look for buses that are heading towards Thiruverumbur or Samayapuram, as these buses will pass by or near the temple. You can board the bus from the nearest bus stop and inform the conductor or driver that you want to get off at Erumbeeswarar Temple.

By Own Vehicle: If you have your own vehicle or are renting one, you can drive to Erumbeeswarar Temple. The temple is located off the Trichy-Chennai Highway (NH38). You can use GPS navigation or follow signboards to reach the temple.
