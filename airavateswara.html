Airavateswara Temple

About Airavateswara Temple

UNESCO World Heritage Site

The Airavateswara Temple, dedicated to Lord Shiva, is one of the three Great Living Chola Temples and is recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Located in Darasuram near Kumbakonam, Tamil Nadu, this temple is renowned for its architectural beauty and intricate stone carvings.

History

Pazhamudhir Cholai Murugan Temple, situated in the scenic hills of Pazhamudhir Cholai near Madurai, is one of the revered temples dedicated to Lord Murugan. This ancient temple has a rich history that dates back several centuries and is believed to have been built during the Tamil Sangam period.

The temple is famous for its legend, which states that Lord Murugan, after defeating the demon Surapadman, rested here. The name "Pazhamudhir Cholai" translates to "the place of ripened fruits," symbolizing the abundance of nature and the divine blessings of Lord Murugan. The temple is surrounded by lush greenery, making it a popular pilgrimage site for devotees seeking solace and divine blessings.

Ways to Reach

Pazhamudhir Cholai Murugan Temple is accessible by various means of transportation:

  • By Air: Madurai International Airport is the nearest airport, located approximately 25 kilometers from the temple. Taxis and auto-rickshaws are available for hire from the airport.
  • By Train: The nearest major railway station is Madurai Junction, situated around 12 kilometers from the temple. You can take a taxi or auto-rickshaw to reach the temple from the station.
  • By Bus: Regular bus services operate from Madurai city to Pazhamudhir Cholai. You can catch a local bus from the city to the temple area.

Festivals

Pazhamudhir Cholai Murugan Temple celebrates several important festivals that draw a large number of devotees:

  • Thai Poosam: This festival is celebrated with great fervor, marking the occasion when Parvati presented the Vel (spear) to Lord Murugan to defeat the demon Soorapadman. Special poojas and processions take place during this time.
  • Chithirai Thiruvizha: Celebrated in the Tamil month of Chithirai, this festival commemorates the marriage of Lord Murugan and Deivayanai, with grand celebrations and rituals.
  • Skanda Shashti: A significant festival dedicated to Lord Murugan, observed for six days, involving various poojas and celebrations to honor the deity's victory over evil.

Pooja Timings

The daily pooja timings at Pazhamudhir Cholai Murugan Temple are as follows:

  • Morning Pooja: 6:00 AM - The morning pooja involves offering prayers and flowers to Lord Murugan.
  • Mid-Morning Pooja: 10:00 AM - A special pooja for devotees visiting in the morning.
  • Evening Pooja: 6:00 PM - A grand aarti and pooja held in the evening.
  • Night Pooja: 8:00 PM - The final pooja of the day before the temple closes.

On festival days, special poojas and rituals are performed, attracting a large number of devotees.
