HISTORY

Perur Pateeswarar Temple, also known as Perur Temple, is a famous Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Shiva. It is located in Coimbatore and is one of the oldest temples in the region. The temple is known for its exquisite architecture and intricate stone carvings. It has a rich history and is considered a sacred pilgrimage site by devotees. The presiding deity, Lord Pateeswarar, is worshipped with great reverence, and the temple attracts a large number of devotees throughout the year.

WAYS TO REACH

By Car/Taxi: If you have your own vehicle or prefer a taxi, you can reach the temple by road. Use GPS or navigation apps to find the best route. The temple is located in Perur, which is approximately 9 kilometers west of Coimbatore city center.

By Bus: Coimbatore has a well-connected public bus network operated by the Tamil Nadu State Transport Corporation (TNSTC). You can check the TNSTC website or inquire at the nearest bus station for routes that pass through Perur. Get off at the appropriate bus stop near Perur Patteeswarar Temple.

By Auto-Rickshaw: Auto-rickshaws are a common mode of transportation in Coimbatore. You can hire an auto-rickshaw from any part of the city and ask the driver to take you to Perur Patteeswarar Temple in Perur.

By Train: The nearest railway station to Perur Patteeswarar Temple is Coimbatore Junction (Coimbatore Main Station). From there, you can hire a taxi or an auto-rickshaw to reach Perur, which is about 9 kilometers from the station.
