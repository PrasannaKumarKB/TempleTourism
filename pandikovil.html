HISTORY

Pandi Temple is an ancient temple located in Melamadai city of Madurai , Tamil Nadu . Pandi Muneeswarar is worshiped as the source of this temple . Pandi Muneeswarar, one of the Pandyan kings , is believed to have resided here.

WAYS TO REACH

By Air: The nearest airport to Madurai is the Madurai International Airport (IXM). From the airport, you can hire a taxi or use public transportation to reach the temple. The temple is located approximately 17 kilometers away from the airport, and the journey takes around 30-40 minutes by road.

By Train: Madurai Junction is the main railway station in Madurai, well-connected to various cities in India. You can take a train to Madurai and then hire a taxi, auto-rickshaw, or use public transport to reach the temple. The Pandi Kovil Temple is situated about 4 kilometers away from the railway station, and it takes around 15-20 minutes to reach by road.

By Bus: Madurai has a well-developed bus transportation system, with both state-run and private buses connecting different cities and towns. You can take a bus to Madurai and get down at the Periyar Bus Stand or the Mattuthavani Integrated Bus Terminus. From there, you can hire a taxi, auto-rickshaw, or use local buses to reach the Pandi Kovil Temple. The temple is located about 7 kilometers away from the bus stands, and the journey may take around 20-30 minutes by road.

By Car: If you prefer to drive, you can reach Madurai by road via national highways. The city is well-connected to major cities and towns in South India. You can use navigation apps or maps to find the best route to the Pandi Kovil Temple.

Once you are in Madurai, you can ask locals or use GPS navigation to guide you to the temple. The Pandi Kovil Temple is a significant religious site in Madurai, so it is relatively well-known, and people can guide you to its location.
