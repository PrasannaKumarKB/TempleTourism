HISTORY

Jambukeswarar Temple, also known as Thiruvanaikaval Temple, is a famous Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Shiva. It is located in Thiruvanaikaval, near Trichy, Tamil Nadu. The temple dates back to ancient times and is one of the five major Shiva temples in Tamil Nadu. The presiding deity, Lord Jambukeswarar (another form of Lord Shiva), is worshipped with great reverence and devotion.

WAYS TO REACH

By Auto-rickshaw or Taxi: The most convenient way to reach the Jambukeswarar Temple is by hiring an auto-rickshaw or a taxi. The temple is located in the Thiruvanaikaval area, which is approximately 5 kilometers from the center of Trichy. You can find auto-rickshaws and taxis easily in Trichy, and they can take you directly to the temple entrance.

By Bus: Trichy has a well-developed bus network, and you can take a local bus to reach the Jambukeswarar Temple. The Thiruvanaikaval Bus Stand is located nearby, and buses that pass through that area will drop you within walking distance of the temple.

By Foot: If you are staying in the central part of Trichy, you can choose to walk to the Jambukeswarar Temple if you enjoy walking and the distance is manageable for you. It would take approximately 45 minutes to 1 hour to reach the temple on foot from the center of Trichy.

By Cycle or Two-Wheeler: Renting a bicycle or a two-wheeler is another option to reach the Jambukeswarar Temple. Trichy has rental services available, and you can explore the city at your own pace, including visiting the temple.
