Eachanari Vinayagar Temple

About Eachanari Temple

Historical Temple

Eachanari Temple, located in Coimbatore, is a revered temple dedicated to Lord Ganesha. This temple is famous for its beautiful architecture and is a significant pilgrimage site for devotees seeking the blessings of Lord Ganesha. Believed to have been constructed in the 19th century, Eachanari Temple is known for its intricate carvings and serene ambiance, making it a popular destination for both local and visiting devotees, especially during the festival of Vinayaka Chaturthi.

History

The Eachanari Temple is believed to have been established in the early 20th century, dedicated to Lord Ganesha. It has a rich history and has undergone several renovations, with contributions from numerous devotees over the years, solidifying its place as an important pilgrimage site in Coimbatore.

The temple is known for its significance in local folklore and is a major attraction during the festival of Vinayaka Chaturthi, when thousands of devotees flock to seek the blessings of Lord Ganesha, especially for new beginnings and removing obstacles in their lives.

Ways to Reach

Eachanari Temple is conveniently accessible by various modes of transportation, ensuring a smooth journey for both local and international visitors:

  • By Air: Coimbatore International Airport is the nearest airport, located about 10 kilometers from the temple. From the airport, you can hire a taxi or use public transport to reach the temple.
  • By Train: Coimbatore Junction Railway Station is the major railhead, situated approximately 8 kilometers from the temple. From the station, you can take a taxi, auto-rickshaw, or use local bus services to reach the temple.
  • By Bus: Eachanari Bus Stop is nearby, with regular bus services operating from various parts of Coimbatore city to the temple area.
  • By Auto-Rickshaw: Auto-rickshaws are widely available in Coimbatore and can be a convenient way to reach Eachanari Temple from different parts of the city.

Festivals

Eachanari Temple is renowned for its vibrant festivals, celebrated with great fervor and devotion. Some of the major festivals include:

  • Panguni Uthiram: Celebrated in the Tamil month of Panguni (March-April), this festival features elaborate processions and rituals dedicated to Lord Ganesh.
  • Vinayaka Chaturthi: This festival, dedicated to Lord Ganesh, involves colorful celebrations, processions, and special poojas, attracting numerous devotees.
  • Navaratri: This nine-day festival dedicated to Goddess Durga is celebrated with various cultural programs, special poojas, and beautifully decorated temple interiors.
  • Thai Poosam: A significant festival for devotees of Lord Murugan, celebrated with fervor, special offerings, and rituals throughout the temple.

Pooja Timings

The daily pooja timings at Eachanari Temple are as follows:

  • Ganesh Pooja (First Pooja): 5:30 AM - The first pooja of the day, where offerings and prayers are made to Lord Ganesh.
  • Kaala Santhi Pooja: 8:00 AM - Morning pooja with rituals and offerings dedicated to the deity.
  • Uchikala Pooja (Noon Pooja): 12:00 PM - Midday pooja where special offerings are made to Lord Ganesh.
  • Sayaraksha Pooja (Evening Pooja): 5:00 PM - Evening pooja with aarti and prayers as the day comes to an end.
  • Ardhajama Pooja (Night Pooja): 9:00 PM - The final pooja of the day before the deity is put to rest.

Special poojas and rituals are performed on auspicious days and during festivals, attracting large numbers of devotees.
