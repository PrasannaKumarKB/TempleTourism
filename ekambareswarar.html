Ekambareswarar Temple

About Ekambareswarar Temple

Historical Temple

Ekambareswarar Temple, located in the ancient city of Kanchipuram near Chennai, is a prominent temple dedicated to Lord Shiva. This temple is one of the five Pancha Bhoota Sthalas, representing the element of Earth. Renowned for its grandeur and unique architecture, Ekambareswarar Temple features a massive gopuram (gateway tower) and exquisite sculptures that reflect the rich cultural heritage of Tamil Nadu. Believed to have been constructed over a thousand years ago, the temple attracts thousands of devotees and tourists every year, especially during festivals and special rituals.

History

Ekambareswarar Temple is believed to have been constructed during the Pallava dynasty, with significant expansions by the Chola dynasty. Renowned for its architectural magnificence, it is one of the five Pancha Bhoota Sthalams, representing the element of Earth. The temple holds great significance as a pilgrimage site for Shaivites and is famously associated with the revered saint, Appar, who is known for his devotional hymns dedicated to Lord Shiva.

Ways to Reach

Ekambareswarar Temple is conveniently accessible by various modes of transportation, ensuring a smooth journey for both local and international visitors:

  • By Air: Chennai International Airport is the nearest airport, located about 70 kilometers from the temple in Kanchipuram. From the airport, you can hire a taxi or use public transport to reach the temple.
  • By Train: The nearest major railway station is Kanchipuram Railway Station, situated approximately 3 kilometers from the temple. You can take a taxi or auto-rickshaw from the station to reach the temple.
  • By Bus: Kanchipuram Bus Terminus is the primary bus station, located around 2 kilometers from the temple. Regular bus services operate from various parts of Chennai to Kanchipuram.
  • By Metro: The nearest metro station is in Chennai. From there, you can take a train or bus to Kanchipuram and then use local transport to reach the temple.

Festivals

Ekambareswarar Temple is renowned for its vibrant festivals, celebrated with immense fervor and devotion. Some of the major festivals include:

  • Panguni Uthiram: Celebrated in the Tamil month of Panguni (March-April), this festival features grand processions and special poojas dedicated to Lord Shiva, along with various religious rituals.
  • Mahashivaratri: This festival is dedicated to Lord Shiva, marked by night-long vigils, special poojas, and offerings, attracting thousands of devotees to the temple.
  • Navaratri: This nine-day festival dedicated to Goddess Durga includes special poojas, cultural programs, and beautifully decorated temple interiors, celebrating the divine feminine.
  • Karthigai Deepam: Known as the festival of lights, Karthigai Deepam is celebrated by lighting traditional oil lamps around the temple, creating a stunning visual experience.

Pooja Timings

The daily pooja timings at Ekambareswarar Temple are as follows:

  • Ko Pooja (First Pooja): 5:30 AM - The first pooja of the day, where offerings and prayers are made to Lord Shiva.
  • Kaala Santhi Pooja: 8:00 AM - Morning pooja with rituals and offerings dedicated to the deity.
  • Uchikala Pooja (Noon Pooja): 12:00 PM - Midday pooja where special offerings are made to Lord Shiva.
  • Sayaraksha Pooja (Evening Pooja): 5:00 PM - Evening pooja with aarti and prayers as the day comes to an end.
  • Ardhajama Pooja (Night Pooja): 9:00 PM - The final pooja of the day before the deity is put to rest.

Special poojas and rituals are performed on auspicious days and during festivals, attracting large numbers of devotees.
