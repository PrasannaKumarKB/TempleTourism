HISTORY

Ekambareswarar Temple, Chennai is a Hindu temple situated in the neighbourhood of Parry's corner (George Town), Chennai, India dedicated to Lord Shiva. It was constructed by Alanganatha Pillai, chief merchant of the Madras factory of the British East India Company.The Ekambareswarar Temple was constructed by Alanganatha Pillai, a dubash in the service of the British East India Company in the 1680s.The temple is marked in a 1710 map of Madras city as "Allingall's Pagoda".

WAYS TO REACH

By Car/Taxi: If you have your own vehicle or prefer a taxi, you can reach the temple by road. Use GPS or navigation apps to find the best route. The temple has parking facilities available nearby.

By Bus: Chennai has an extensive public bus network operated by the Metropolitan Transport Corporation (MTC). You can check the MTC website or inquire at the nearest bus station for the routes that pass through Chennai Central and alight at Ekambareswarar Temple bus stop.

By Metro: Chennai Metro Rail provides a convenient option to reach Ekambareswarar Temple. You can take the metro and get down at the "High Court" metro station, which is located near the temple. From there, you can either walk or take a short auto-rickshaw ride to reach the temple.

By Train: The nearest railway station to Ekambareswarar Temple is the Chennai Railway Station. From there, you can hire a taxi or take an auto-rickshaw to reach the temple, which is approximately 1 kilometer away.

By Auto-Rickshaw: Auto-rickshaws are readily available in Chennai. You can hire an auto-rickshaw from any part of the city and ask the driver to take you to Ekambareswarar Temple.
