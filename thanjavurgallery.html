FAMOUS TOURIST PLACES IN AND AROUND THANJAVUR

Brihadeeswarar Temple

A UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the largest temples in India, dedicated to Lord Shiva. It is renowned for its architectural grandeur and intricate stone carvings.

Thanjavur Palace

A historic palace complex built by the Nayak and Maratha rulers, featuring museums that display a vast collection of artifacts, including royal weaponry, costumes, and artworks.

Swamimalai Murugan Temple

A prominent temple dedicated to Lord Murugan, known for its unique architecture and significance as one of the six abodes of Lord Murugan.

Poompuhar Beach

A serene beach located on the Bay of Bengal, known for its golden sands, picturesque sunsets, and ancient port ruins that showcase the rich maritime history of the region.

Airavatesvara Temple

A stunning temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, renowned for its intricate stone carvings and exquisite architecture. It is also a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Thiruvaiyaru

A town known for its association with Saint Thyagaraja, one of the greatest composers of Carnatic music. The annual Thyagaraja Aradhana music festival held here attracts renowned musicians and music enthusiasts.

Thanjavur Art Gallery and Saraswathi Mahal Library

A treasure trove of art and knowledge, the art gallery houses exquisite paintings, sculptures, and bronze artifacts, while the library boasts a vast collection of ancient manuscripts and books.

Gangaikonda Cholapuram

A UNESCO World Heritage Site and an ancient temple town established by the Chola dynasty, known for the Brihadisvara Temple, which exhibits architectural brilliance.

Karikalan Chola Statue

A statue dedicated to Karikalan, one of the legendary Chola kings, located on the banks of the Kaveri River. It commemorates the contributions of the Chola dynasty to the region.
