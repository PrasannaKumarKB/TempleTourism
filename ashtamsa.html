HISTORY

Ashtamsa Varadha Anjaneyar Temple is a revered Hindu temple located in Coimbatore, Tamil Nadu. It is dedicated to Lord Hanuman, also known as Anjaneyar. The temple is believed to have a significant historical and spiritual importance. Devotees visit this temple to seek the blessings of Lord Hanuman and experience a sense of peace and divine energy.

WAYS TO REACH

By Car/Taxi: If you have your own vehicle or prefer a taxi, you can reach the temple by road. Use GPS or navigation apps to find the best route. The temple is located in R.S. Puram, Coimbatore.

By Bus: Coimbatore has a well-connected public bus network operated by the Tamil Nadu State Transport Corporation (TNSTC). You can check the TNSTC website or inquire at the nearest bus station for routes that pass through R.S. Puram. Get off at the appropriate bus stop near the Ashtamsa Varadha Anjaneyar Temple.

By Train: Coimbatore is well-connected by train to major cities in India. The nearest railway station to the Ashtamsa Varadha Anjaneyar Temple is Coimbatore Junction (Coimbatore Main Station). From there, you can hire a taxi or an auto-rickshaw to reach R.S. Puram and the temple.

By Auto-Rickshaw: Auto-rickshaws are a convenient mode of transportation in Coimbatore. You can hire an auto-rickshaw from any part of the city and ask the driver to take you to the Ashtamsa Varadha Anjaneyar Temple in R.S. Puram.
