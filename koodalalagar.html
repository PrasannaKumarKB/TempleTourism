Koodal Alagar Temple

History

Koodal Alagar Temple, located in the heart of Madurai, is dedicated to Lord Vishnu and is an ancient temple with a rich history. Believed to have been constructed during the 7th century CE, it holds immense significance in the Vaishnavite tradition. The temple's name, "Koodal Alagar," translates to "the one who is seated in the assembly," reflecting Lord Vishnu's divine presence in Madurai.

This temple is known for its exquisite architecture, vibrant sculptures, and intricate carvings that narrate various episodes from Hindu mythology. Koodal Alagar is a prominent pilgrimage site, especially during the Tamil month of Chithirai when the annual festival of Alagar Kalyanam is celebrated with grandeur.

Ways to Reach

Koodal Alagar Temple is conveniently accessible by various means of transportation:

  • By Air: Madurai International Airport is the nearest airport, located about 12 kilometers from the temple. Taxis and auto-rickshaws are available for hire from the airport.
  • By Train: The nearest major railway station is Madurai Junction, which is approximately 3 kilometers away. Local transport options such as taxis, auto-rickshaws, and buses can be used to reach the temple from the station.
  • By Bus: The temple is well-connected by local buses, and several services operate from various parts of the city, making it easy for visitors to reach.

Festivals

Koodal Alagar Temple hosts several vibrant festivals that draw large crowds of devotees. Major festivals include:

  • Alagar Kalyanam: The most important festival celebrated in the Tamil month of Chithirai, this festival commemorates the divine wedding of Lord Alagar and Goddess Valli, featuring elaborate rituals and processions.
  • Panguni Uthiram: This festival marks the celestial marriage of Lord Murugan and is celebrated with great enthusiasm, involving various religious ceremonies and rituals.
  • Vaikunta Ekadashi: A significant festival for Vaishnavites, celebrated with special poojas and festivities during the month of Margazhi.

Pooja Timings

The daily pooja timings at Koodal Alagar Temple are as follows:

  • Morning Pooja: 6:00 AM - The early morning pooja, where devotees offer prayers and flowers to Lord Alagar.
  • Mid-Morning Pooja: 10:00 AM - A special pooja held for devotees visiting in the morning.
  • Evening Pooja: 6:00 PM - The evening aarti, a vibrant and devotional ritual that draws many attendees.
  • Night Pooja: 8:00 PM - The final pooja of the day, marking the end of the temple activities.

On festival days and auspicious occasions, special rituals and ceremonies are conducted to honor the deities.
