HISTORY

Koodal Aḻagar Temple in Madurai, a city in the South Indian state of Tamil Nadu, is dedicated to the Hindu god Vishnu. Constructed in the Dravidian style of architecture, the temple is glorified in the Naalayira Divya Prabandham, the early medieval Tamil canon of the Alvar saints from the 6th–9th centuries CE. It is one of the 108 Divya Desams dedicated to Vishnu, who is worshipped as Viyooga Sundarrajan, and his consort Lakshmi as Mathuravalli.A granite wall surrounds the temple, enclosing all its shrines. The temple has a five-tiered rajagopuram, the gateway tower. The temple is originally believed to be built by the Pandyas, with later additions by the Vijayanagara empire and Madurai Nayaks kings who commissioned pillared halls and major shrines of the temple during the 16th century.Koodal Aḻagar is believed to have appeared to slay the demon Somuka who abducted the four Vedas. The temple follows the Tenkalai tradition of worship. Four daily rituals and three yearly festivals are held at the temple, of which the fourteen-day annual Brahmotsavam during the Tamil month of Vaikasi (May - June) being the most prominent.

WAYS TO REACH

By Air: The nearest airport to Madurai is the Madurai International Airport (IXM). From the airport, you can hire a taxi or use public transportation to reach the temple. The temple is approximately 15 kilometers away from the airport, and the journey takes around 30 minutes by road.

By Train: Madurai Junction is a major railway station well-connected to various cities in India. You can take a train to Madurai and then hire a taxi, auto-rickshaw, or use public transport to reach the temple. The temple is located about 3 kilometers away from the railway station, and it takes around 10-15 minutes to reach by road.

By Bus: Madurai has a well-developed bus transportation system, and you can find both state-run and private buses connecting various cities and towns. You can take a bus to Madurai and get down at the Periyar Bus Stand or the Mattuthavani Integrated Bus Terminus. From there, you can hire a taxi, auto-rickshaw, or use local buses to reach the Shri Koodal Alagar Temple.

By Car: If you prefer to drive, you can reach Madurai by road via national highways. The city is well-connected to major cities and towns in South India. You can use navigation apps or maps to find the best route to the Shri Koodal Alagar Temple.
