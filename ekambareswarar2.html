HISTORY

The Ekambareswarar Temple is a Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, located in Kanchipuram, Tamil Nadu, India. It is one of the Pancha Bhoota Stalas representing the element Earth. The temple is believed to have been built in the 7th century CE and is one of the largest temples in India. The main deity, Lord Shiva, is worshipped in the form of a lingam known as Prithvi Lingam or Ekambareswarar. The temple complex is spread over 25 acres and consists of several shrines, mandapams, and gopurams.
