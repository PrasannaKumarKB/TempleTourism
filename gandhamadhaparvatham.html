HISTORY

Gandhamadhana Parvatham Temple is a sacred Hindu temple located in Rameshwaram, Tamil Nadu. It is situated on Gandhamadhana Parvatham hill, which is believed to be the place where Lord Rama placed his feet to get a view of Lanka before his journey to rescue his wife, Sita, from the demon king Ravana. The temple is dedicated to Lord Rama and is visited by devotees seeking his blessings and offering prayers.

WAYS TO REACH

By Road: Gandhamadhanaparvatham Temple is located on the Pamban Island in Rameshwaram. You can hire a taxi or an auto-rickshaw from Rameshwaram town and drive towards Pamban Island. The temple is situated on top of a hill, and there is a road that leads to the temple entrance. You can park your vehicle at the designated parking area and then climb the steps to reach the temple.

By Bicycle or Two-wheeler: If you prefer a more adventurous approach, you can rent a bicycle or a two-wheeler in Rameshwaram and ride to Pamban Island. There is a bridge connecting Rameshwaram to Pamban Island, known as Pamban Bridge. You can ride across the bridge and reach the island. From there, follow the signs or ask the locals for directions to Gandhamadhanaparvatham Temple.

By Foot: For those who enjoy trekking or walking, you can also reach Gandhamadhanaparvatham Temple by foot. The temple is approximately 3 kilometers from the Ramanathaswamy Temple in Rameshwaram town. You can walk along the beach or take the road leading to Pamban Island. It's a scenic walk, and you can enjoy the views along the way.
