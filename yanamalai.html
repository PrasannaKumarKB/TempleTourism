Yoga Narashimhar Temple

History

The Yoga Narasimhar Temple, located in the picturesque Yanamalai hills near Madurai, is dedicated to Lord Narasimha, an incarnation of Lord Vishnu. The temple has a rich heritage, believed to have been constructed several centuries ago by devotees seeking the divine blessings of Lord Narasimha.

This temple is known for its serene environment and stunning natural surroundings, making it a popular pilgrimage site. Devotees visit to seek the blessings of Lord Narasimha, who is worshipped here in his yoga posture, symbolizing tranquility and strength.

Ways to Reach

Yoga Narasimhar Temple is accessible through various modes of transportation:

  • By Air: The nearest airport is Madurai International Airport, situated approximately 20 kilometers from the temple. Taxis and auto-rickshaws are available for hire from the airport.
  • By Train: Madurai Junction is the closest major railway station, about 10 kilometers away. Local transport, such as taxis and auto-rickshaws, can be used to reach the temple from the station.
  • By Bus: Regular bus services operate to Yanamalai from various parts of Madurai, making it easy for visitors to access the temple.

Festivals

Yoga Narasimhar Temple celebrates several festivals that attract numerous devotees. Major festivals include:

  • Narasimha Jayanti: This festival celebrates the birth of Lord Narasimha with special poojas, rituals, and the chanting of sacred hymns.
  • Vaikunta Ekadasi: A significant day for devotees, marked by elaborate rituals and offerings to Lord Narasimha, drawing a large number of worshippers.
  • Panguni Uthiram: Celebrated with great enthusiasm, this festival involves various special poojas and processions.

Pooja Timings

The daily pooja timings at Yoga Narasimhar Temple are as follows:

  • Morning Pooja: 6:30 AM - The first pooja of the day, where offerings are made to Lord Narasimha.
  • Afternoon Pooja: 12:00 PM - Midday rituals for devotees visiting the temple.
  • Evening Pooja: 6:30 PM - The evening aarti, invoking the blessings of Lord Narasimha.
  • Night Pooja: 8:00 PM - The final pooja of the day before the temple closes.

On festival days and special occasions, additional poojas and rituals are performed to enhance the spiritual experience for devotees.
