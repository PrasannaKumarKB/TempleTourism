HISTORY

Ramanathaswamy Temple is an ancient Hindu temple located in Rameshwaram, Tamil Nadu. It is dedicated to Lord Shiva and is considered one of the holiest shrines in Hinduism. According to mythology, this temple was built by Lord Rama and is one of the twelve Jyotirlinga temples. The temple's history is deeply intertwined with the Ramayana epic and attracts millions of pilgrims and tourists every year.

WAYS TO REACH

By Air: The nearest airport to Rameshwaram is Madurai International Airport, which is well-connected to major cities in India. From the airport, you can hire a taxi or take a bus to Rameshwaram, which is approximately 3 hours away.

By Train: Rameshwaram has its own railway station, which is well-connected to major cities in India. You can take a train to Rameshwaram Railway Station and then hire an auto-rickshaw or taxi to reach the temple.

By Road: Rameshwaram is well-connected by road network. You can drive to Rameshwaram from nearby cities or hire a taxi. The temple is located in the center of Rameshwaram town and is easily accessible by road.

By Bus: State-owned and private buses operate regular services to Rameshwaram from various cities in Tamil Nadu and neighboring states. You can check the bus schedules and book a ticket to reach Rameshwaram. Once you arrive at the bus stand in Rameshwaram, you can hire an auto-rickshaw or taxi to reach the temple.

By Ferry: If you are coming from the mainland, you can also take a ferry from Mandapam, which is located around 20 kilometers from Rameshwaram. The ferry ride takes approximately 15-20 minutes to reach the temple. It's a unique and scenic way to reach Rameshwaram.
