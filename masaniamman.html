Masani Amman Temple

History

Masani Amman Temple, located in Pollachi, Tamil Nadu, is a renowned temple dedicated to Goddess Masani Amman, a powerful and revered deity. The temple's origins are steeped in local folklore and spiritual beliefs, with the deity believed to protect her devotees from evil forces and grant them justice. The temple has been a symbol of faith and justice for centuries.

Historically, the temple has drawn thousands of devotees, particularly those who believe in the power of Goddess Masani to resolve conflicts and grant justice. Ways to Reach

Masani Amman Temple is easily accessible by various modes of transportation, ensuring a convenient journey for visitors from nearby and far-off locations:

  • By Air: Coimbatore International Airport is the nearest airport, located about 55 kilometers from the temple. From the airport, taxis and buses are available to Pollachi.
  • By Train: Pollachi Junction is the closest railway station, situated around 15 kilometers from the temple. Auto-rickshaws, taxis, and buses can be used to reach the temple from the station.
  • By Bus: Pollachi Bus Stand is about 15 kilometers away. Regular bus services from various parts of Tamil Nadu and Kerala make Pollachi a well-connected destination.

Festivals

Masani Amman Temple is known for celebrating several important festivals with grandeur, drawing large crowds of devotees. Some of the key festivals include:

  • Masani Amman Festival: Celebrated annually during the Tamil month of Thai (January-February), this festival involves special rituals, poojas, and processions dedicated to the goddess.
  • Aadi Perukku: Celebrated during the Tamil month of Aadi (July-August), this festival involves worshipping the river goddess and special rituals at the temple to ensure prosperity.
  • Navaratri: The nine-day festival is dedicated to the worship of Goddess Durga, where special poojas are held, and the temple is beautifully decorated.

Pooja Timings

The daily pooja schedule at Masani Amman Temple is as follows:

  • Morning Pooja: 6:00 AM - The first pooja of the day, with offerings and prayers to Goddess Masani Amman.
  • Mid-Morning Pooja: 10:00 AM - A special pooja conducted for devotees visiting in the morning hours.
  • Evening Pooja: 6:00 PM - A grand aarti and pooja during the sunset, invoking the goddess's blessings.
  • Night Pooja: 8:00 PM - The final pooja of the day before the temple closes for the night.

On special occasions and festival days, additional rituals and offerings are performed to honor the goddess.
