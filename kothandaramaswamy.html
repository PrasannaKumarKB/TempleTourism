HISTORY

Kothandaramaswamy Temple is an ancient Hindu temple located in Rameshwaram, Tamil Nadu. It is believed to be the place where Lord Rama stayed and worshiped Lord Shiva after rescuing his wife, Sita, from the demon king Ravana. The temple is dedicated to Lord Rama and is an important pilgrimage site for devotees. It has historical significance and attracts visitors from all over the country.

WAYS TO REACH

By Road: Kothandaramaswamy Temple is located on the Dhanushkodi Road, around 12 kilometers away from the Rameshwaram town. You can hire a taxi or an auto-rickshaw from Rameshwaram and drive towards Dhanushkodi Road. The temple is situated on the way to Dhanushkodi, and you can easily spot it on the right side of the road.

By Local Bus: Rameshwaram has local bus services that connect various parts of the town, including the Kothandaramaswamy Temple. You can inquire about the bus routes and take a bus from Rameshwaram to the temple. Make sure to check the bus timings and schedules in advance.

By Auto-rickshaw: Auto-rickshaws are a popular mode of transportation in Rameshwaram. You can hire an auto-rickshaw from Rameshwaram and ask the driver to take you to Kothandaramaswamy Temple. They are familiar with the route and can drop you off at the temple entrance.

By Bicycle or Two-wheeler: Another option is to rent a bicycle or a two-wheeler in Rameshwaram and ride to the Kothandaramaswamy Temple. This allows you to enjoy the scenic beauty of the area while reaching your destination.
