Parthasarathy Temple

About Parthasarathy Temple

Historical Temple

Parthasarathy Temple, located in Triplicane, Chennai, is one of the oldest temples dedicated to Lord Krishna, known here as Parthasarathy. The temple's architecture is a classic example of Dravidian style, with intricate carvings and impressive gopurams (gateway towers), attracting devotees and tourists alike.

History

The Parthasarathy Temple was built during the Pallava dynasty in the 8th century AD. It has since undergone various renovations by different dynasties, including the Chola and Vijayanagara empires. The temple's name 'Parthasarathy' means the charioteer of Arjuna, reflecting Lord Krishna's role in the Mahabharata.

Ways to Reach

Parthasarathy Temple is easily accessible from various parts of Chennai:

  • By Air: The nearest airport is Chennai International Airport, around 20 km from the temple. Taxis and ride-sharing services are available from the airport.
  • By Train: Chennai Central and Egmore Railway Stations are the closest major railway stations, about 5 km away. Local trains also connect to Triplicane Railway Station, which is a short walk from the temple.
  • By Bus: The temple is well-connected by city buses. The nearest bus stop is Triplicane, serviced by several routes.
  • By Car: You can use GPS services to navigate to Parthasarathy Temple. Parking is available near the temple premises.

Festivals

Parthasarathy Temple is renowned for its vibrant festivals, which are celebrated with great fervor and devotion. Some of the major festivals include:

  • Theppam (Float) Festival: Celebrated in the Tamil month of Masi (February-March), this festival features the deity being placed on a beautifully decorated float and taken around the temple tank in a grand procession.
  • Vaikunta Ekadasi: Celebrated in December-January, this is a major festival where the gates of Vaikunta are believed to open. Devotees participate in special prayers, and the temple's "Sorga Vaasal" is opened for darshan.
  • Brahmotsavam: A 10-day festival in the Tamil month of Chithirai (April-May) that includes processions of the deity on various vahanas (vehicles) like Garuda and Hanuman, with cultural performances and religious rites.
  • Pavitrotsavam: Conducted in Aavani (August-September), this festival involves purification rituals, special prayers, and the ceremonial cleansing of the temple.
  • Rama Navami: Celebrated in Panguni (March-April), this festival marks the birth of Lord Rama, with special poojas, processions, and cultural programs at the temple.

Pooja Timings

The daily pooja timings at Parthasarathy Temple are as follows:

  • Vishwaroopa Darshanam: 6:00 AM - Early morning darshan where the deity is woken up with chants and offerings.
  • Thirupavai Pooja: 6:30 AM - Recitation of the Thirupavai hymns dedicated to Lord Vishnu.
  • Kala Santhi Pooja: 8:00 AM - Morning pooja with elaborate offerings and rituals performed by the priests.
  • Uchikala Pooja (Noon Pooja): 12:00 PM - Midday pooja with special offerings to the deity.
  • Sayaratchai Pooja (Evening Pooja): 6:00 PM - Evening pooja with aarti and offerings.
  • Ardha Jama Pooja (Night Pooja): 9:00 PM - The final pooja of the day, where the deity is put to rest.

Special poojas and rituals are performed on auspicious days and during festivals, drawing large gatherings of devotees.
