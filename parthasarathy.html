Parthasarathy Temple

About Parthasarathy Temple

Historical Temple

Parthasarathy Temple, located in Triplicane, Chennai, is one of the oldest temples dedicated to Lord Krishna, known here as Parthasarathy. The temple's architecture is a classic example of Dravidian style, with intricate carvings and impressive gopurams (gateway towers), attracting devotees and tourists alike.

History

The Parthasarathy Temple was built during the Pallava dynasty in the 8th century AD. It has since undergone various renovations by different dynasties, including the Chola and Vijayanagara empires. The temple's name 'Parthasarathy' means the charioteer of Arjuna, reflecting Lord Krishna's role in the Mahabharata.

Ways to Reach

Parthasarathy Temple is easily accessible from various parts of Chennai:

  • By Air: The nearest airport is Chennai International Airport, around 20 km from the temple. Taxis and ride-sharing services are available from the airport.
  • By Train: Chennai Central and Egmore Railway Stations are the closest major railway stations, about 5 km away. Local trains also connect to Triplicane Railway Station, which is a short walk from the temple.
  • By Bus: The temple is well-connected by city buses. The nearest bus stop is Triplicane, serviced by several routes.
  • By Car: You can use GPS services to navigate to Parthasarathy Temple. Parking is available near the temple premises.
