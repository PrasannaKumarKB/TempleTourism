HISTORY

The Vaikunta Perumal Temple, also known as the Kanchi Varadaraja Perumal Temple, is a famous Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Vishnu. Located in Kanchipuram, Tamil Nadu, India, it is one of the 108 Divya Desams, the holy abodes of Lord Vishnu. The temple is known for its magnificent architecture and intricate carvings. It was built during the Pallava dynasty and later expanded by the Vijayanagara and Chola dynasties. The main deity, Lord Varadaraja Perumal, is depicted in a reclining posture on the great serpent Adisesha. The temple complex also houses several other shrines dedicated to various deities.
