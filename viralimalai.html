HISTORY

Viralimalai Murugan Temple is a renowned Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Murugan (Subramanya). It is located in the town of Viralimalai, near Trichy in Tamil Nadu, India. The temple is situated atop a hillock known as Viralimalai, which translates to "Hill of the White Peacock." According to legend, Lord Murugan assumed the form of a peacock and vanquished the demon Surapadman at this spot. The temple has a rich historical significance and showcases beautiful architecture and intricate carvings. Devotees visit the temple to seek blessings from Lord Murugan and offer prayers for fulfillment of their wishes and removal of obstacles.

WAYS TO REACH

By Road: Viralimalai Murugan Temple is located in the town of Viralimalai, approximately 30 kilometers from Trichy. You can reach the temple by road using various modes of transportation such as:

Taxi or Car: Hiring a taxi or driving in your own car is a convenient option to reach the temple. You can take the Trichy-Pudukkottai Highway (NH38) and follow the signs towards Viralimalai.

Bus: Regular bus services operate from Trichy to Viralimalai. You can take a bus from Trichy Central Bus Stand (Chatram Bus Stand) that goes towards Viralimalai. The journey takes approximately one hour.

By Train: Viralimalai has its own railway station, known as Viralimalai Railway Station. However, the frequency of trains might be limited. Alternatively, you can take a train from Trichy Junction Railway Station to Pudukkottai Railway Station and then travel to Viralimalai by road, which is approximately 20 kilometers away.

By Air: The nearest airport to Viralimalai is Tiruchirappalli International Airport (TRZ) in Trichy. From the airport, you can hire a taxi or take a bus to reach Viralimalai Murugan Temple.
