HISTORY

Vedapureeswarar Temple is a Hindu temple dedicated to the deity Shiva, located in Thiruverkadu, a Municipality in Tiruvallur district in the South Indian state of Tamil Nadu. Shiva is worshipped as Vedapureeswarar, and is represented by the lingam. His consort Parvati is depicted as Balambigai. The presiding deity is revered in the 7th century Tamil Saiva canonical work, the Tevaram, written by Tamil saint poets known as the Nayanars and classified as Paadal Petra Sthalam. The temple complex covers two acres and it houses two gateway towers known as gopurams, each facing the Vedapureeswarar and Balambigai shrine. The temple has a number of shrines, with those of Vedapureeswarar and his consort Balambigai being the most prominent. The temple has six daily rituals at various times from 6:00 a.m. to 8:30 p.m., and four yearly festivals on its calendar. The Brahmotsavam festival is celebrated during the day of the Magam (February - March) is the most prominent festival.

WAYS TO REACH

By Car/Taxi: If you have your own vehicle or prefer a taxi, you can reach the temple by road. Thiruverkadu is located in the northwestern part of Chennai, and you can use GPS or navigation apps to find the best route to the temple. The temple has parking facilities available nearby.

By Bus: Chennai has an extensive public bus network operated by the Metropolitan Transport Corporation (MTC). You can check the MTC website or inquire at the nearest bus station for the routes that pass through Thiruverkadu and alight at the Thiruverkadu Bus Stand. From there, the temple is within walking distance.

By Metro: Currently, there is no metro connectivity to Thiruverkadu. The nearest metro station is Koyambedu, and from there, you can take a bus, taxi, or auto-rickshaw to reach Thiruverkadu.

By Train: The nearest railway station to Thiruverkadu is Avadi Railway Station. From Avadi, you can hire a taxi or take a bus or auto-rickshaw to reach the Sri Vedapureeswarar Temple in Thiruverkadu.

By Auto-Rickshaw: Auto-rickshaws are readily available in Chennai. You can hire an auto-rickshaw from any part of the city and ask the driver to take you to Sri Vedapureeswarar Temple in Thiruverkadu.
