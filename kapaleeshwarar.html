Kapaleeshwarar Temple

About Kapaleeshwarar Temple

Historical Temple

Kapaleeshwarar Temple, located in the vibrant neighborhood of Mylapore in Chennai, is one of the most revered and ancient temples in South India. Dedicated to Lord Shiva, this architectural marvel is a fine example of Dravidian style, adorned with intricate carvings, towering gopurams (gateway towers), and a rich history that dates back to the 7th century CE.

History

The temple's origins can be traced back to the Pallava dynasty, with significant contributions from the Vijayanagara Empire in later centuries. The name 'Kapaleeshwarar' is derived from the words 'Kapalam' (head) and 'Ishwara' (Lord Shiva), linked to a legend where Lord Shiva, in the form of Kapaleeshwarar, performed penance to atone for a sin.

The temple complex houses numerous shrines, each with its unique history and significance. The major deities worshipped here are Kapaleeshwarar (Lord Shiva) and Karpagambal (Goddess Parvati), drawing devotees from all over the world.

Ways to Reach

Kapaleeshwarar Temple is conveniently accessible by various modes of transportation, ensuring a smooth journey for both local and international visitors:

  • By Air: Chennai International Airport is the nearest airport, located about 18 kilometers from the temple. From the airport, you can hire a taxi or use public transport to reach the temple.
  • By Train: Chennai Central Railway Station is the major railhead, situated approximately 7 kilometers from the temple. From the station, you can take a taxi, auto-rickshaw, or use local bus services to reach the temple.
  • By Bus: Chennai Mofussil Bus Terminus (CMBT) is the primary bus station, located around 12 kilometers from the temple. Regular bus services operate from CMBT to various parts of the city, including Mylapore.
  • By Metro: The nearest metro station to the temple is Thirumayilai Metro Station, which is just 1 kilometer away. You can either walk or take an auto-rickshaw from the metro station to the temple.

Festivals

Kapaleeshwarar Temple is renowned for its vibrant festivals, which are celebrated with great fervor and devotion. Some of the major festivals include:

  • Panguni Peruvizha: Celebrated in the Tamil month of Panguni (March-April), this festival is marked by grand processions, cultural events, and various religious rituals.
  • Arubathimoovar Festival: Dedicated to the 63 Nayanmars (saints) of Lord Shiva, this festival involves a magnificent procession of the saints' idols through the streets of Mylapore.
  • Navaratri: This nine-day festival dedicated to Goddess Durga is celebrated with special poojas, cultural programs, and beautifully decorated temples.
  • Karthigai Deepam: Known as the festival of lights, Karthigai Deepam is celebrated by lighting traditional oil lamps around the temple premises, creating a mesmerizing sight.

Pooja Timings

The daily pooja timings at Kapaleeshwarar Temple are as follows:

  • Ko Pooja (First Pooja): 5:30 AM - The first pooja of the day, where offerings and prayers are made to Lord Shiva.
  • Kaala Santhi Pooja: 8:00 AM - Morning pooja with rituals and offerings.
  • Uchikala Pooja (Noon Pooja): 12:00 PM - Midday pooja where special offerings are made to the deity.
  • Sayaraksha Pooja (Evening Pooja): 5:00 PM - Evening pooja with aarti and chants as the day comes to an end.
  • Ardhajama Pooja (Night Pooja): 9:00 PM - The final pooja of the day before the deity is put to rest.

Special poojas and rituals are performed on auspicious days and during festivals, attracting large numbers of devotees.
